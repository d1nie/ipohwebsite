Culture and Heritage

Colonial Legacy

Ipoh Railway Station

This majestic Moorish-style station, built in 1917, is a stunning example of colonial architecture and serves as a gateway to the city's heritage.

Birch Memorial Clock Tower

This controversial landmark, erected in 1909, commemorates the first British resident of Perak, James Birch, who was assassinated by Malay chiefs.

Ipoh Town Hall

This grand neoclassical building, constructed in 1916, now houses the Perak State Museum and Art Gallery, showcasing the region's history and culture

Chinese Heritage

Kek Lok Tong Cave Temple

This impressive cave temple, dating back to 1926, features intricate Buddhist sculptures and stunning stalactite formations

Perak Tong Temple

This majestic Taoist temple, perched on a limestone hill, offers panoramic views of the city and houses a collection of over 1,200 Buddha statues

Concubine Lane

This narrow alleyway, once home to wealthy Chinese merchants and their mistresses, is now lined with charming shophouses and trendy cafes.

Indian Heritage

Sri Mariamman Temple

This impressive cave temple, dating back to 1926, features intricate Buddhist
sculptures and stunning stalactite formationsThis ornate Hindu temple, built in 1890, is
the oldest Hindu temple in Ipoh and is known for its colorful

Street Art

Mural Art's Lane

This vibrant alleyway is adorned with colorful murals by local and international artists, showcasing Ipoh's creative spirit and street art scene

Ipoh Art Alley

This hidden gem features a collection of murals depicting scenes of Ipoh's past and present, offering a glimpse into the city's unique character
